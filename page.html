About Me

Hi, I'm Pearl! I'm a PhD graduate with expertise in data analysis, visualization, and modeling, leveraging tools like R and Python. My research has focused on longitudinal and cross-sectional data, particularly in sleep and neurodevelopmental risks.

I have hands-on experience in:

  • Data Wrangling: Automating data extraction, cleaning, and transformation pipelines with tidyr, dplyr, and pandas.
  • Statistical Analysis:Applied techniques like latent growth curve modeling, structural equation modeling, and predictive analytics for complex datasets (e.g., time-series)
  • Visualization: Creating engaging and actionable insights using ggplot2, seaborn, and matplotlib.
  • Big Data Tools: Experienced in working with SQL databases and processing large-scale datasets efficiently.
    • I've also mentored research assistants in data analysis, guided their independent projects, and supported their career growth.

In my free time, I enjoy yoga, jewelry-making, and experimenting with creative projects.
